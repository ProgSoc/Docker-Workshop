Rivalry Erupts: Inside the Saja Boys vs HUNTR/X K-Pop Drama

By K-Pop Reporter Sarah Kim | October 1, 2025 | 3 hours ago

In what has become the most explosive K-pop rivalry since the fan wars of 2024, rising boy group Saja Boys and soloist HUNTR/X have taken their competition to unprecedented levels, leaving the K-pop community stunned and divided.

The faces behind the rivalry: Saja Boys (top) and HUNTR/X (bottom)

The conflict, which began as friendly competition over music show wins, escalated dramatically last week when HUNTR/X publicly challenged the Saja Boys' latest comeback on social media, claiming their choreography was "recycled moves from 2015" and their vocals were "heavily auto-tuned."

"We've seen K-pop rivalries before," says music critic Ji-Young Park, "but nothing quite like this. The Saja Boys have built their reputation on synchronized perfection and group chemistry, while HUNTR/X represents the rising solo artist mentality. It's a clash of philosophies as much as egos."

The Saja Boys Strike Back

The Saja Boys, never ones to back down from a challenge, responded with a devastating dance practice video that went viral across social media. Their flawless 8-minute performance, posted with the caption "This is how it's done," showcased what fans called "legendary synchronization" and "vocals that don't need fixing."

"Look, we respect solo artists," said one Saja Boys member during a V Live stream. "But when you come at us, you better bring your A-game. And frankly, HUNTR/X showed up unprepared."

HUNTR/X Fires Back

HUNTR/X, however, was far from defeated. In a shocking move, they released an impromptu live performance video— singing and dancing simultaneously without any backing track. The video peaked at over 10 million views in 24 hours and sparked fierce debates about raw talent versus polished group performances.

"The Saja Boys hide behind their numbers," HUNTR/X stated in an Instagram story. "When you're solo, every move matters. Every note is yours. No relying on others to carry the chorus. Just pure, unfiltered talent."

Fandom Wars Intensify

The K-pop community has split into camps, with #TeamSaja and #TeamHUNTRX trending worldwide across multiple platforms. YouTube comments have devolved into flame wars, and several music shows have had to implement special security protocols to prevent confrontations between fan groups.

"This is getting out of hand," admits fan community moderator Min-Ji Lee. "We've had to ban discussions of the rivalry on three major K-pop Discord servers. Fans are literally unfollowing friends based on which side they support."

What's Next?

Both acts have been confirmed for next month's MAMA Awards 2025, where they'll perform back-to-back in what's being called the "Battle of the Year." The anticipation has already broken pre-voting records, with both fandoms mobilizing globally to support their favorites.

Whatever the outcome, one thing is certain: the K-pop world will never be the same. As one Twitter user aptly put it, "Forget BTS vs EXO. This is the real rivalry of our generation."

Editor's Note: K-Pop Insider reached out to both the Saja Boys and HUNTR/X's agencies for official comment. The Saja Boys' agency responded with a formal statement: "Our artists speak through their performances." HUNTR/X's agency sent a GIF of their artist doing a mic drop, followed by "See you at MAMA."
